## Section 2: The Problem We Solve

### Section Label
THE PROBLEM

### Headline
Millions of Hours Lost to a Spreadsheet That Should Fill Itself

### Body Copy
Every day, finance teams, operations managers, and data analysts sit down to do the same thing: open a PDF, look at the numbers, and type them into a spreadsheet. Invoice by invoice. Row by row. Page by page.

It's slow. It's error-prone. And it scales with headcount, not with ambition — the more documents you process, the more people you need doing it.

The problem isn't the people. It's the process. PDFs were never designed to talk to spreadsheets, and until now, there was no reliable way to bridge that gap without manual effort.

Organizations lose thousands of hours annually to this workflow. Errors compound — a misread digit on an invoice, a missed row in a report, a field copied to the wrong column — and the cost shows up in audits, reconciliations, and decisions made on bad data.

LNA was built specifically to solve this. Not with a workaround. Not with a complicated configuration process. With genuine AI that reads documents the way you would — and outputs structured, accurate, exportable data automatically.

## Section 4: Our Values

### Section Label
WHAT WE STAND FOR

### Section Headline
The Principles Behind Every Decision We Make

---

### Value 1: Accuracy First
Data that can't be trusted is worse than no data at all. We build confidence scoring, audit trails, and verification mechanisms into everything LNA does — because your decisions depend on getting it right.

---

### Value 2: AI with Purpose
We don't add AI features because they're impressive. We add them because they solve a real problem. Every AI capability in LNA — from schema-free extraction to context roles — exists because it makes your work meaningfully better.

---

### Value 3: Customer-Centric by Default
Our customers are people who process documents for a living. We build for their actual workflows — the templates they've already created, the formats their systems expect, the questions they need to ask of their data. LNA fits around how you work, not the other way around.

---

### Value 4: Transparency in Every Output
LNA shows its work. Every extracted field carries a confidence score. Every export includes an audit trail. We believe that automation should be explainable — so you're never in the dark about what the AI did and why.

---

### Value 5: Continuous Innovation
The technology behind document AI is evolving fast, and so are the needs of the teams we serve. We're committed to releasing meaningful improvements consistently — not as marketing moments, but as genuine expansions of what LNA can do for you.

## Copy Notes for Development

### About Page
- Team member cards should use placeholder avatar images in a consistent style (monogram initials or silhouette) until real photos are provided. Avoid leaving empty image containers.
- The "Problem We Solve" section can be broken into a two-column layout with a visual element (e.g., a document-to-spreadsheet illustration) on one side and the text on the other.
- Values can be displayed as an icon grid (4 or 5 cards, 2 columns on desktop) or as a vertical list with large typographic numbering.
- The Manureva Solutions section should link to the company's main website once available.

### Contact Page
- The form should include inline validation for required fields and email format.
- On mobile, the sidebar collapses below the form.
- The dropdown default state should read "Select a reason..." rather than defaulting to option 1.
- The confirmation message should display in place of the form after successful submission — do not redirect to a separate page.
- If a "Demo Request" is selected in the dropdown, consider adding a conditional field for "Preferred date/time" or surfacing the Calendly link inline.
